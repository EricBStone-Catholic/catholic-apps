The Holy Rosary

A Complete Guide for Every Day

Today’s Mysteries
The Glorious Mysteries
Opening

Begin by making the Sign of the Cross:

In the name of the Father,
and of the Son,
and of the Holy Spirit.
Amen.
Apostles’ Creed

Hold the crucifix and pray:

I believe in God, the Father almighty,
Creator of heaven and earth,
and in Jesus Christ, his only Son, our Lord,
who was conceived by the Holy Spirit,
born of the Virgin Mary,
suffered under Pontius Pilate,
was crucified, died and was buried;
he descended into hell;
on the third day he rose again from the dead;
he ascended into heaven,
and is seated at the right hand of God the Father almighty;
from there he will come to judge the living and the dead.
I believe in the Holy Spirit,
the holy catholic Church,
the communion of saints,
the forgiveness of sins,
the resurrection of the body,
and life everlasting.
Amen.

The Joyful Mysteries

Monday & Saturday

First MysteryThe Annunciation

The Angel Gabriel announces to Mary that she will conceive the Son of God, and she responds with perfect trust: “Be it done unto me according to thy word.”

Fruit of this Mystery
Humility

Our Father — ten Hail Marys — Glory Be — O my Jesus…

Second MysteryThe Visitation

Mary visits her cousin Elizabeth, who is filled with the Holy Spirit and recognizes the Lord in Mary’s womb.

Fruit of this Mystery
Love of Neighbor

Our Father — ten Hail Marys — Glory Be — O my Jesus…

Third MysteryThe Nativity

Jesus, the Son of God, is born in a stable in Bethlehem, wrapped in swaddling clothes and laid in a manger.

Fruit of this Mystery
Poverty of Spirit / Detachment

Our Father — ten Hail Marys — Glory Be — O my Jesus…

Fourth MysteryThe Presentation

Mary and Joseph present the infant Jesus in the Temple, fulfilling the Law, and Simeon prophesies that a sword will pierce Mary’s own soul.

Fruit of this Mystery
Obedience

Our Father — ten Hail Marys — Glory Be — O my Jesus…

Fifth MysteryThe Finding in the Temple

After three days of searching, Mary and Joseph find the twelve-year-old Jesus in the Temple, seated among the teachers, about his Father’s business.

Fruit of this Mystery
Zeal for the Things of God

Our Father — ten Hail Marys — Glory Be — O my Jesus…

The Sorrowful Mysteries

Tuesday & Friday

First MysteryThe Agony in the Garden

Jesus prays in Gethsemane, so anguished that his sweat falls like drops of blood, yet he submits perfectly to the Father’s will.

Fruit of this Mystery
Contrition / Conformity to God’s Will

Our Father — ten Hail Marys — Glory Be — O my Jesus…

Second MysteryThe Scourging at the Pillar

Jesus is bound to a pillar and scourged by Roman soldiers, his body torn by their blows.

Fruit of this Mystery
Mortification of the Senses

Our Father — ten Hail Marys — Glory Be — O my Jesus…

Third MysteryThe Crowning with Thorns

A crown of thorns is pressed onto Jesus’s head and he is mocked as King of the Jews.

Fruit of this Mystery
Moral Courage

Our Father — ten Hail Marys — Glory Be — O my Jesus…

Fourth MysteryThe Carrying of the Cross

Jesus carries his Cross through the streets of Jerusalem to Calvary, falling beneath its weight.

Fruit of this Mystery
Patience

Our Father — ten Hail Marys — Glory Be — O my Jesus…

Fifth MysteryThe Crucifixion

Jesus is nailed to the Cross and dies for the sins of the world, giving his mother to the beloved disciple and commending his spirit to the Father.

Fruit of this Mystery
Salvation / Perseverance

Our Father — ten Hail Marys — Glory Be — O my Jesus…

The Glorious Mysteries

Wednesday & Sunday

First MysteryThe Resurrection

Jesus rises from the dead on the third day, triumphant over sin and death, appearing first to Mary Magdalene in the garden.

Fruit of this Mystery
Faith

Our Father — ten Hail Marys — Glory Be — O my Jesus…

Second MysteryThe Ascension

Forty days after his Resurrection, Jesus ascends to heaven in the sight of his disciples, promising to send the Holy Spirit.

Fruit of this Mystery
Hope / Desire for Heaven

Our Father — ten Hail Marys — Glory Be — O my Jesus…

Third MysteryThe Descent of the Holy Spirit

The Holy Spirit descends upon Mary and the Apostles gathered in the upper room at Pentecost, filling them with fire and courage.

Fruit of this Mystery
Gifts of the Holy Spirit

Our Father — ten Hail Marys — Glory Be — O my Jesus…

Fourth MysteryThe Assumption

At the end of her earthly life, Mary is taken body and soul into heavenly glory by the power of God.

Fruit of this Mystery
Grace of a Happy Death

Our Father — ten Hail Marys — Glory Be — O my Jesus…

Fifth MysteryThe Coronation of Mary

Mary is crowned Queen of Heaven and Earth by her Son, interceding for all humanity before the throne of God.

Fruit of this Mystery
Trust in Mary’s Intercession

Our Father — ten Hail Marys — Glory Be — O my Jesus…

The Luminous Mysteries

Thursday

First MysteryThe Baptism of Jesus

Jesus is baptized in the Jordan by John, and the Father’s voice proclaims him the beloved Son as the Holy Spirit descends like a dove.

Fruit of this Mystery
Openness to the Holy Spirit

Our Father — ten Hail Marys — Glory Be — O my Jesus…

Second MysteryThe Wedding at Cana

At Mary’s quiet intercession, Jesus performs his first miracle, turning water into wine and revealing his glory to his disciples.

Fruit of this Mystery
Fidelity / Trust in Mary’s Intercession

Our Father — ten Hail Marys — Glory Be — O my Jesus…

Third MysteryThe Proclamation of the Kingdom

Jesus calls all to repentance and proclaims the Good News of God’s Kingdom, forgiving sins and healing the broken-hearted.

Fruit of this Mystery
Repentance / Trust in God

Our Father — ten Hail Marys — Glory Be — O my Jesus…

Fourth MysteryThe Transfiguration

Jesus is transfigured in blinding glory on Mount Tabor before Peter, James, and John, revealing his divine nature.

Fruit of this Mystery
Desire for Holiness

Our Father — ten Hail Marys — Glory Be — O my Jesus…

Fifth MysteryThe Institution of the Eucharist

At the Last Supper, Jesus gives us his Body and Blood under the forms of bread and wine, instituting the Eucharist and the priesthood.

Fruit of this Mystery
Eucharistic Adoration

Our Father — ten Hail Marys — Glory Be — O my Jesus…

Hail Holy Queen
Hail, Holy Queen, Mother of Mercy,
our life, our sweetness and our hope.
To thee do we cry, poor banished children of Eve;
to thee do we send up our sighs,
mourning and weeping in this valley of tears.
Turn then, most gracious advocate,
thine eyes of mercy toward us,
and after this our exile,
show unto us the blessed fruit of thy womb, Jesus.
O clement, O loving, O sweet Virgin Mary.
Concluding Prayer

The priest or leader prays:

O God, whose only-begotten Son,
by his life, death, and resurrection,
has purchased for us the rewards of eternal life,
grant, we beseech thee,
that meditating upon these mysteries
of the most Holy Rosary of the Blessed Virgin Mary,
we may imitate what they contain
and obtain what they promise,
through the same Christ our Lord.
Amen.
Sign of the Cross
In the name of the Father,
and of the Son,
and of the Holy Spirit.
Amen.
Prayer to St. Michael

Prayed after the Rosary in many parishes, by request of Pope Leo XIII:

Saint Michael the Archangel,
defend us in battle.
Be our protection against the wickedness
and snares of the devil.
May God rebuke him, we humbly pray;
and do thou, O Prince of the Heavenly Host,
by the power of God,
cast into hell Satan and all the evil spirits
who prowl about the world
seeking the ruin of souls.
Amen.
