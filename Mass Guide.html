PART I
Liturgy of the Word

Introductory Rites

All stand as the priest and ministers process in. The Sign of the Cross begins Mass.

Sign of the Cross
PriestIn the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit.
PeopleAmen.
Greeting

The priest uses one of several greetings; the most common:

PriestThe Lord be with you.
PeopleAnd with your spirit.
As a Lutheran you said "And also with you" — Catholics say "And with your spirit." This is a more literal translation of the Latin et cum spiritu tuo.
Penitential Act

The priest invites the assembly to call to mind their sins. One of three forms is used. Form A (the Confiteor) is most common at Sunday Mass:

Confiteor (All say together, striking breast at through my fault):

I confess to almighty God
and to you, my brothers and sisters,
that I have greatly sinned,
in my thoughts and in my words,
in what I have done and in what I have failed to do,
through my fault, through my fault,
through my most grievous fault;
therefore I ask blessed Mary ever-Virgin,
all the Angels and Saints,
and you, my brothers and sisters,
to pray for me to the Lord our God.
PriestMay almighty God have mercy on us, forgive us our sins, and bring us to everlasting life.
PeopleAmen.
· · ·

On some days, especially feasts and Sundays, Form C (Kyrie) is used instead — a beautiful ancient exchange:

CantorLord, have mercy.
PeopleLord, have mercy.
CantorChrist, have mercy.
PeopleChrist, have mercy.
CantorLord, have mercy.
PeopleLord, have mercy.
CantorKyrie, eleison.
PeopleKyrie, eleison.
CantorChriste, eleison.
PeopleChriste, eleison.
CantorKyrie, eleison.
PeopleKyrie, eleison.
When the Confiteor is used, the Kyrie usually follows it. When sung, each phrase is typically repeated twice or three times.
Gloria

Said or sung on Sundays (outside Advent and Lent) and on solemnities. Omitted during Lent and Advent. Returns with great solemnity on Holy Thursday.

Glory to God in the highest,
and on earth peace to people of good will.

We praise you,
we bless you,
we adore you,
we glorify you,
we give you thanks for your great glory,
Lord God, heavenly King,
O God, almighty Father.

Lord Jesus Christ, Only Begotten Son,
Lord God, Lamb of God, Son of the Father,
you take away the sins of the world, have mercy on us;
you take away the sins of the world, receive our prayer;
you are seated at the right hand of the Father,
have mercy on us.

For you alone are the Holy One,
you alone are the Lord,
you alone are the Most High,
Jesus Christ,
with the Holy Spirit,
in the glory of God the Father.
Amen.
Gloria in excelsis Deo
et in terra pax hominibus bonae voluntatis.

Laudamus te,
benedicimus te,
adoramus te,
glorificamus te,
gratias agimus tibi propter magnam gloriam tuam,
Domine Deus, Rex caelestis,
Deus Pater omnipotens.

Domine Fili unigenite, Iesu Christe,
Domine Deus, Agnus Dei, Filius Patris,
qui tollis peccata mundi, miserere nobis;
qui tollis peccata mundi, suscipe deprecationem nostram;
qui sedes ad dexteram Patris, miserere nobis.

Quoniam tu solus Sanctus,
tu solus Dominus,
tu solus Altissimus,
Iesu Christe,
cum Sancto Spiritu:
in gloria Dei Patris.
Amen.
The Gloria is omitted throughout Lent and Advent — a silence that makes its return all the more powerful. When you do not hear it on Sundays during this season, its absence is itself an act of liturgical preparation.
On Holy Thursday — the Mass of the Lord’s Supper — the Gloria returns after its Lenten silence. As it is sung, the church bells ring out joyfully for the last time until the Easter Vigil. It is one of the most dramatic moments in the entire liturgical year. Be ready for it.

Jesus Christ,

Amen.
Collect (Opening Prayer)
PriestLet us pray. (pause for silent prayer) … [prays the Collect aloud] … through Christ our Lord.
PeopleAmen.

All sit.

Liturgy of the Word
Readings

Typically: First Reading (OT) → Responsorial Psalm → Second Reading (NT, Sundays) → Gospel Acclamation → Gospel

LectorA reading from… [reads] … The word of the Lord.
PeopleThanks be to God.
Responsorial Psalm

The cantor sings the psalm. The response is printed in the bulletin or projected. You repeat the refrain after the cantor sings it the first time, then after each verse.

Gospel Acclamation

All stand. The cantor sings the acclamation and the people repeat it.

AllAlleluia, Alleluia, Alleluia.

The cantor sings a verse; the people repeat the Alleluia.

AllPraise to you, Lord Jesus Christ, King of endless glory.

The Alleluia is not sung during Lent. The cantor sings a verse; the people repeat the acclamation. Other common Lenten verses: "Your words, Lord, are spirit and life." — "A clean heart create for me, O God."

Gospel
PriestThe Lord be with you.
PeopleAnd with your spirit.
PriestA reading from the holy Gospel according to N.
PeopleGlory to you, O Lord.

At these words, trace a small cross with your thumb on your forehead, lips, and heart — signifying: may this Gospel be in my mind, on my lips, and in my heart.

Priest[reads Gospel] … The Gospel of the Lord.
PeoplePraise to you, Lord Jesus Christ.

All sit for the Homily.

Nicene Creed

All stand. Said on Sundays and solemnities. At the words and by the Holy Spirit was incarnate, all bow.

I believe in one God,
the Father almighty,
maker of heaven and earth,
of all things visible and invisible.

I believe in one Lord Jesus Christ,
the Only Begotten Son of God,
born of the Father before all ages.
God from God, Light from Light,
true God from true God,
begotten, not made, consubstantial with the Father;
through him all things were made.
For us men and for our salvation
he came down from heaven,

(all bow:)
and by the Holy Spirit was incarnate of the Virgin Mary,
and became man.

For our sake he was crucified under Pontius Pilate,
he suffered death and was buried,
and rose again on the third day
in accordance with the Scriptures.
He ascended into heaven
and is seated at the right hand of the Father.
He will come again in glory
to judge the living and the dead
and his kingdom will have no end.

I believe in the Holy Spirit, the Lord, the giver of life,
who proceeds from the Father and the Son,
who with the Father and the Son is adored and glorified,
who has spoken through the prophets.

I believe in one, holy, catholic and apostolic Church.
I confess one Baptism for the forgiveness of sins
and I look forward to the resurrection of the dead
and the life of the world to come. Amen.
As a Lutheran you know the Apostles' Creed. The Nicene Creed is longer and more theologically precise. "Consubstantial" is the key word — it means the Son is of the same divine substance as the Father.
Prayer of the Faithful (Universal Prayer)

The deacon or a lector reads intercessions. The response varies by parish — commonly:

Lector…we pray to the Lord. (or similar)
PeopleLord, hear our prayer.

PART II
Liturgy of the Eucharist

All sit as the gifts are brought to the altar.

Preparation of the Gifts (Offertory)

The priest prepares the bread and wine. After each, if said aloud (not all parishes do this):

PriestBlessed are you, Lord God of all creation… Blessed be God for ever.
PeopleBlessed be God for ever.
PriestPray, brethren (brothers and sisters), that my sacrifice and yours may be acceptable to God, the almighty Father.
PeopleMay the Lord accept the sacrifice at your hands for the praise and glory of his name, for our good and the good of all his holy Church.

All stand.

Priest[Prayer over the Offerings] … through Christ our Lord.
PeopleAmen.
Eucharistic Prayer
Preface Dialogue
PriestThe Lord be with you.
PeopleAnd with your spirit.
PriestLift up your hearts.
PeopleWe lift them up to the Lord.
PriestLet us give thanks to the Lord our God.
PeopleIt is right and just.
This ancient exchange (Sursum corda) dates to at least the 3rd century. "It is right and just" replaced the older translation "It is right to give him thanks and praise" in 2011.

The priest sings or says the Preface (a prayer of thanksgiving). It ends leading into the Sanctus:

Sanctus (Holy, Holy, Holy)
Holy, Holy, Holy Lord God of hosts.
Heaven and earth are full of your glory.
Hosanna in the highest.
Blessed is he who comes in the name of the Lord.
Hosanna in the highest.
Sanctus, Sanctus, Sanctus
Dominus Deus Sabaoth.
Pleni sunt caeli et terra gloria tua.
Hosanna in excelsis.
Benedictus qui venit in nomine Domini.
Hosanna in excelsis.

All kneel after the Sanctus (in most parishes).

Words of Institution / Consecration

The priest prays over the bread and wine. At the elevation of each, bells may ring. This is the most sacred moment of Mass — Catholics believe the bread and wine truly become the Body and Blood of Christ.

PriestThe mystery of faith.
The Memorial Acclamation — one of three options your parish uses. The most common:
AllWe proclaim your Death, O Lord, and profess your Resurrection until you come again.

Other options your parish may use:

"When we eat this Bread and drink this Cup, we proclaim your Death, O Lord, until you come again."

"Save us, Savior of the world, for by your Cross and Resurrection you have set us free."

Concluding Doxology
PriestThrough him, and with him, and in him, O God, almighty Father, in the unity of the Holy Spirit, all glory and honor is yours, for ever and ever.
PeopleAmen.
This "Great Amen" is the most important Amen in the Mass — the people's assent to the entire Eucharistic Prayer. Sung congregations often sing it boldly three times.
Communion Rite
Lord's Prayer
PriestAt the Savior's command and formed by divine teaching, we dare to say:
Our Father, who art in heaven,
hallowed be thy name;
thy kingdom come,
thy will be done
on earth as it is in heaven.
Give us this day our daily bread,
and forgive us our trespasses,
as we forgive those who trespass against us;
and lead us not into temptation,
but deliver us from evil.
Catholics stop here — the doxology ("For thine is the kingdom…") is NOT said or sung in the Roman Rite. The priest then continues with an embolism prayer, and the people respond:
Priest…as we await the blessed hope and the coming of our Savior, Jesus Christ.
PeopleFor the kingdom, the power and the glory are yours now and for ever.
Sign of Peace
Priest…The peace of the Lord be with you always.
PeopleAnd with your spirit.
PriestLet us offer each other the sign of peace.

Turn and greet those near you: "Peace be with you." They respond: "And with you" or "And also with you" — informally, whatever feels natural.

Agnus Dei (Lamb of God)

The priest breaks the consecrated host (the Fraction). The congregation sings or says:

Lamb of God, you take away the sins of the world, have mercy on us.
Lamb of God, you take away the sins of the world, have mercy on us.
Lamb of God, you take away the sins of the world, grant us peace.
Agnus Dei, qui tollis peccata mundi, miserere nobis.
Agnus Dei, qui tollis peccata mundi, miserere nobis.
Agnus Dei, qui tollis peccata mundi, dona nobis pacem.
Communion

The priest genuflects and holds up the host:

PriestBehold the Lamb of God, behold him who takes away the sins of the world. Blessed are those called to the supper of the Lamb.
AllLord, I am not worthy that you should enter under my roof, but only say the word and my soul shall be healed.
This echoes the centurion in Matthew 8:8. The 2011 translation restored "under my roof" — the older translation was "Lord, I am not worthy to receive you…"

As you approach the minister, bow your head. The minister holds up the host:

MinisterThe Body of Christ. (and/or: The Blood of Christ.)
YouAmen.
This "Amen" is your personal act of faith — you are affirming belief in the Real Presence. Make it count. After receiving, return to your pew and kneel for a time of private prayer. This is one of the most treasured moments in Catholic practice.
Prayer after Communion

All stand. The priest says the prayer:

Priest…through Christ our Lord. (or similar ending)
PeopleAmen.
Concluding Rites
PriestThe Lord be with you.
PeopleAnd with your spirit.

The priest gives the final blessing. If a deacon or bishop is present, he may give it. The people bow their heads:

PriestMay almighty God bless you: the Father, and the Son, ✛ and the Holy Spirit.
PeopleAmen.
Dismissal

The priest or deacon uses one of four formulas — the response is always the same:

PriestGo forth, the Mass is ended.
PeopleThanks be to God.
PriestGo and announce the Gospel of the Lord.
PeopleThanks be to God.
PriestGo in peace, glorifying the Lord by your life.
PeopleThanks be to God.
PriestGo in peace.
PeopleThanks be to God.
The word "Mass" comes from the Latin Ite, missa est — "Go, it is sent" or "Go, you are dismissed." The entire liturgy takes its name from this sending forth.

Prayer to St. Michael

Prayed after Mass in many parishes, by request of Pope Leo XIII:

Saint Michael the Archangel,
defend us in battle.
Be our protection against the wickedness
and snares of the devil.
May God rebuke him, we humbly pray;
and do thou, O Prince of the Heavenly Host,
by the power of God,
cast into hell Satan and all the evil spirits
who prowl about the world
seeking the ruin of souls.
Amen.
The Angelus

An ancient devotion commemorating the Incarnation, traditionally prayed at morning, noon, and evening. Prayed after weekday Mass at the MCB Quantico Chapel — a fitting close for those who serve, reminding us that the Word became flesh and dwelt among us.

PriestThe Angel of the Lord declared unto Mary,
PeopleAnd she conceived of the Holy Spirit.
Hail Mary, full of grace, the Lord is with thee.
Blessed art thou among women,
and blessed is the fruit of thy womb, Jesus.
Holy Mary, Mother of God,
pray for us sinners,
now and at the hour of our death.
Amen.
PriestBehold the handmaid of the Lord,
PeopleBe it done unto me according to thy word.
Hail Mary… (as above)
PriestAnd the Word was made flesh,
PeopleAnd dwelt among us.
Hail Mary… (as above)
PriestPray for us, O Holy Mother of God,
PeopleThat we may be made worthy of the promises of Christ.

The priest concludes with the Collect:

Pour forth, we beseech thee, O Lord,
thy grace into our hearts,
that we to whom the Incarnation of Christ thy Son
was made known by the message of an Angel,
may by his Passion and Cross
be brought to the glory of his Resurrection.
Through the same Christ our Lord.
Amen.
